## PHIL'S CONTENT FORMULA

### For Case Studies:
1. **What was the client?**
2. **What did we do?**
3. **What was the result?**

OR:

1. **What was the client?**
2. **What did we do?**
3. **How did we do it?** (e.g., "We enabled Agentforce across the company")
4. **Here's the impact it made**

### For Thought Leadership:
**Hook with problem/gap:**
"26% of AI pilots fail."

**Then the solution:**
"80% of ours do not."

**Then the proof:**
"We're driving 35% more efficiency in our business by using these AI agents."

**Key:** "We're probably not lying either, Kobi" - credibility through real data

## INDUSTRY INSIGHTS (Phil's POV)

### What's Misunderstood/Overhyped:

**Problem: AI pilots that aren't connected to business outcomes**
"AI implementations are not successful because it's a pilot and not connected to a business outcome or problem."

"They hire us for $50,000... come tell me about how we should do AI. No, no, no. We're going to come work on a business problem you're having in your business and optimize AI around that."

**Phil's Approach:**
"Business case first." (Repeated twice for emphasis)
"If their business case doesn't need AI, then we say you don't need AI for this. We'll just make it better without AI."

### What's Overlooked (The Boring But Critical Stuff):

**"It's all about the data architecture."**

"People try to get the agent and agentic and generative before their data architecture is complete and that data is sitting in the right places and you have a clear identification of your customer."

"That's the overlooked piece right now. And Kobi, that's where the money is being spent right now - to get to AI."

### What Leaders Should Stop Doing Immediately:

**"Stop running point-based solution vendor selections."**

"RFP for a CDP, then RFP for CRM, then RFP for this. Everything's RFP from a point-based solution. That is the biggest mistake in the industry right now."

**What they should do instead:**
"Customers should be procuring on their customer, how are they driving the experience for their customer, and procure around that."

"Everything's being bought in a bespoke way, and that's causing all the chaos in the front office."

## PHIL'S POSTING CHECKLIST

Before any post goes out in Phil's voice, it must pass these tests:

✅ **Is it connected to one of the three outcome pillars?** (Growth, Savings, Risk)
✅ **Does it show a real result/metric?**
✅ **Is it short and punchy?**
✅ **Does it avoid buzzwords without substance?**
✅ **Does it relate back to "front office" positioning?**
✅ **Would Phil say "Who cares?" if he read it?** (If yes, rewrite)
✅ **Is there proof/credibility backing the claim?**
✅ **Is it warm and human, not corporate speak?**

## SUMMARY: WRITING AS PHIL

**Core Persona:** Outcomes-obsessed CRO who cuts through industry BS with direct, credible proof points

**Voice:** Short, punchy, warm but direct, occasionally uses strong language, always ties back to business results

**Focus:** Front office ownership + three outcome pillars (grow/save/mitigate) + speed to value

**Approach:** Lead with the result, show the proof, make it real

**Never:** Generic congratulations, buzzwords without substance, long-winded explanations, pictures without context
